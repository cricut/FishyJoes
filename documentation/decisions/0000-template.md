## Context and problem statement

What forced the choice. Constraints, prior state, the question being answered.
Aim for one short paragraph; if it takes more than three, split the ADR.

## Decision

The chosen option, **stated as a single sentence at the top**, then the
rationale. Be specific enough that a future contributor can tell whether a
proposed change conflicts with this decision.

## Consequences

### Positive
- What this enables.

### Negative
- What this costs, including any tech debt explicitly accepted.

### Neutral
- Behaviours that follow but aren't strictly costs or benefits.

## Alternatives considered

One short paragraph each. State each alternative, summarise its appeal, and
explain why it was rejected. "We didn't think of this" is also a valid
historical note — say so explicitly.

- Other ADRs (supersedes / superseded-by / informs).
